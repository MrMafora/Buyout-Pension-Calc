# FERS vs CSRS: How Buyouts Work Differently for Each System

**Published:** February 1, 2026  
**Reading time:** 8 minutes  
**Categories:** Federal Retirement, Buyouts, FERS, CSRS

---

If you're a federal employee facing a buyout offer, understanding how your retirement system affects the calculation is crucial. FERS and CSRS employees see dramatically different results from the same buyout amount.

## The Quick Difference

**FERS employees** typically get smaller pensions but keep their Social Security benefits. **CSRS employees** get larger pensions but may face bigger early retirement penalties if they take a buyout before age 55.

Let's break down exactly how this works.

## Understanding the Two Systems

### FERS (Federal Employees Retirement System)

Launched in 1987, FERS is a three-tiered system:

1. **Basic Benefit Plan** - Your pension
2. **Social Security** - Full benefits at retirement
3. **Thrift Savings Plan (TSP)** - With agency matching

**Pension Formula:**
```
High-3 Salary × Years of Service × 1.0% (or 1.1% if 62+ with 20+ years)
```

### CSRS (Civil Service Retirement System)

The original system (pre-1987), CSRS provides:

1. **Generous Pension** - Higher than FERS
2. **No Social Security** - Unless you have other coverage
3. **TSP** - Available but no agency matching

**Pension Formula (Tiered):**
```
First 5 years: 1.5% per year
Next 5 years: 1.75% per year
All years after 10: 2.0% per year
```

## Buyout Impact: Real Example

Let's look at two employees with identical situations:
- **Age:** 55
- **Years of Service:** 25
- **High-3 Salary:** $80,000
- **Buyout Offer:** $25,000

### FERS Employee

**Pension Calculation:**
- $80,000 × 25 years × 1.0% = **$20,000/year**
- Monthly: **$1,667**

**Plus Social Security at 62:** ~$1,800/month

**Total Retirement Income:** $3,467/month

### CSRS Employee

**Pension Calculation:**
- First 5 years: 5 × 1.5% = 7.5%
- Next 5 years: 5 × 1.75% = 8.75%
- Remaining 15 years: 15 × 2.0% = 30%
- **Total: 46.25% of High-3**
- $80,000 × 46.25% = **$37,000/year**
- Monthly: **$3,083**

**Plus TSP withdrawals:** Variable

**Total Retirement Income:** ~$3,500-4,000/month

## The Buyout Decision

### For FERS Employees

**Advantages of taking buyout:**
- Social Security still available at 62
- TSP continues growing
- Can work private sector without pension penalty
- Often better long-term if you can earn comparable salary

**Calculate your break-even:**
```
Buyout Amount ÷ Monthly Pension = Break-even months
```

For our example: $25,000 ÷ $1,667 = 15 months

**The pension catches up to the buyout in just over a year.**

### For CSRS Employees

**Advantages of taking buyout:**
- Immediate lump sum
- Can invest for potentially higher returns
- Flexibility to change careers

**But watch out for:**
- **Early retirement penalty:** If under 55, pension reduced permanently
- **No Social Security:** Most CSRS employees don't have it
- **Higher dependency on pension:** Larger portion of retirement income

**Calculate your break-even:**

For our example: $25,000 ÷ $3,083 = 8 months

**The pension catches up to the buyout in just 8 months.**

## Special Considerations

### Health Insurance

Both systems keep FEHB if you:
- Take immediate retirement (not deferred)
- Have been enrolled for 5 years before retirement

**Important:** Taking a buyout with deferred retirement means losing FEHB.

### Military Buyback

If you have military service:

**FERS:** Can buy back time to increase pension by 1.0% per year
**CSRS:** Can buy back time at higher rates (up to 2.0% per year)

**Impact:** CSRS employees get more value from military buyback.

### Special Provisions

**Law Enforcement, Firefighters, Air Traffic Controllers:**

Both systems offer enhanced benefits:
- Earlier retirement age
- Higher multipliers (1.7% for FERS first 20 years)
- Different calculation rules

**Critical:** These provisions change buyout calculations significantly.

## Tax Implications

Both systems face the same buyout taxation:

**Federal Tax:** Withheld at 20-25% (depending on total income)
**Social Security:** 6.2% up to wage base
**Medicare:** 1.45%
**State Tax:** Varies (0-13.3%)

**Net result:** You keep roughly 60-70% of the buyout after taxes.

## Making Your Decision

### Questions to Ask

1. **What's your monthly pension amount?** (Calculate it precisely)
2. **How old are you?** (Early retirement penalties apply)
3. **What's your break-even point?** (Months until pension > buyout)
4. **Can you earn comparable income elsewhere?** (Private sector opportunity)
5. **Do you have other retirement savings?** (TSP, IRAs, etc.)

## Bottom Line

**CSRS employees** generally have more to lose from taking a buyout due to higher pension values. The break-even comes faster.

**FERS employees** have more flexibility due to Social Security and TSP, but should carefully calculate the trade-off.

**Both systems:** Run the numbers before deciding. A $25,000 buyout looks attractive until you realize your pension is worth $300,000+ over retirement.
