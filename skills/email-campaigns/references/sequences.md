# Email Sequences Reference

This document defines the standard email sequences for FedBuyOut campaigns.

## Welcome Sequence

Target: New leads who just signed up
Goal: Onboard, build trust, drive to consultation

### Email 1: Welcome (Day 0 - Immediate)

**Subject:** Welcome to FedBuyOut - Here's What to Expect

**Purpose:** Set expectations, introduce the brand, immediate value

**Key Elements:**
- Warm welcome with first name personalization
- What they can expect from the sequence
- Brief overview of FedBuyOut services
- Set expectation: "Over the next few days, we'll share..."
- Single clear CTA: Download guide or book consultation

**Send Time:** Immediate after signup

---

### Email 2: Value Proposition (Day 2)

**Subject:** How Federal Employees Unlock Their Pension Value

**Purpose:** Educate on the core service, address "why buyout?"

**Key Elements:**
- Common pain points federal employees face
- How buyouts solve these problems
- Simple explanation of the process
- Social proof (number of clients helped)
- CTA: Learn more about the process

**Send Time:** 9:00 AM local time

---

### Email 3: Social Proof + CTA (Day 5)

**Subject:** "I Wish I Knew About This Sooner" - Success Stories

**Purpose:** Build trust through testimonials, drive to action

**Key Elements:**
- 2-3 client success stories with specific results
- Before/after scenarios
- Address common objections
- Strong CTA: Schedule your free consultation
- Urgency element: Limited consultation slots

**Send Time:** 9:00 AM local time

---

## Educational Drip Campaign (5 Emails)

Target: Cold/warm leads who need education
Goal: Educate on pension buyouts, build authority, nurture to consultation

### Email 1: Understanding Your Pension Options (Day 0)

**Subject:** Understanding Your Pension: 3 Options You Should Know

**Purpose:** Educate on pension choices without selling

**Content Outline:**
1. Option 1: Traditional monthly payments (pros/cons)
2. Option 2: Lump sum withdrawal (pros/cons)
3. Option 3: Pension buyout (brief intro, save details for later)
4. Key considerations when choosing
5. Soft CTA: "Want to discuss your specific situation?"

**Tone:** Educational, helpful, no pressure

---

### Email 2: The True Cost of Waiting (Day 3)

**Subject:** The Hidden Cost of Waiting on Your Pension Decision

**Purpose:** Create urgency without being pushy

**Content Outline:**
1. How pension values change over time
2. Market factors affecting pension buyout rates
3. Opportunity cost of waiting
4. Case study: Early vs. late decision
5. CTA: Calculate your pension value today

**Tone:** Informative, eye-opening, helpful

---

### Email 3: Buyout vs. Monthly Payments (Day 6)

**Subject:** Pension Buyout vs. Monthly Payments: A Side-by-Side Comparison

**Purpose:** Direct comparison to help decision-making

**Content Outline:**
1. Quick reference comparison table
2. When monthly payments make sense
3. When buyouts make sense
4. Factors to consider (health, goals, risk tolerance)
5. CTA: Get a personalized analysis

**Tone:** Balanced, objective, decision-supportive

---

### Email 4: Tax Implications Explained (Day 9)

**Subject:** Taxes and Your Pension: What Every Federal Employee Needs to Know

**Purpose:** Address tax concerns, show expertise

**Content Outline:**
1. How pension income is taxed
2. Tax implications of buyouts
3. Strategies to minimize tax burden
4. Common tax mistakes to avoid
5. Disclaimer + CTA: Consult with our tax-savvy advisors

**Tone:** Authoritative, detailed, cautionary

---

### Email 5: Next Steps & Consultation Offer (Day 12)

**Subject:** Ready to Discuss Your Options? Here's What's Next

**Purpose:** Convert educated leads to consultations

**Content Outline:**
1. Recap of key learnings from the series
2. When you're ready to take the next step
3. What to expect in a consultation
4. No-pressure guarantee
5. Strong CTA: Book your free consultation (with calendar link)
6. P.S. - Final value proposition

**Tone:** Supportive, professional, action-oriented

---

## Re-engagement Campaign

Target: Leads who haven't opened emails in 30+ days
Goal: Win back or clean from list

### Email 1: We Miss You (Day 0)

**Subject:** We Miss You at FedBuyOut

**Purpose:** Gentle re-engagement attempt

**Content Outline:**
1. Acknowledge absence non-judgmentally
2. Remind of value proposition
3. "What's changed?" section with updates
4. Easy CTA: Still interested? Click here
5. Alternative: Update preferences

**Tone:** Friendly, understanding, not desperate

---

### Email 2: What's Changed? (Day 3)

**Subject:** What's Changed in Pension Buyouts (And Why It Matters)

**Purpose:** Provide fresh value, different angle

**Content Outline:**
1. Industry updates or changes
2. New success story or case study
3. Updated offer or service enhancement
4. "What this means for you"
5. CTA: Learn about recent changes

**Tone:** Informative, fresh, valuable

---

### Email 3: Final Chance (Day 7)

**Subject:** Final Notice: Should We Keep You Updated?

**Purpose:** Last attempt with clear unsubscribe option

**Content Outline:**
1. Direct question about continued interest
2. Quick summary of what they'll miss
3. Two clear options:
   - Yes, keep me subscribed (CTA)
   - No, unsubscribe me (link)
4. Professional closing

**Tone:** Direct, respectful, final

---

## Sequence Timing Best Practices

### Send Days
- **Best:** Tuesday, Wednesday, Thursday
- **Acceptable:** Monday, Friday
- **Avoid:** Saturday, Sunday (unless B2C emergency)

### Send Times
- **9:00 AM** - Start of workday (professional)
- **1:00 PM** - Post-lunch check
- **3:00 PM** - Afternoon break
- **Avoid:** Before 8 AM, after 6 PM

### Frequency Guidelines
- **Welcome:** Immediate, then every 2-3 days
- **Educational:** Every 3 days
- **Re-engagement:** Daily for 3 days (final attempt)
- **Newsletter:** Weekly or bi-weekly

### Engagement Triggers
- Opened but no click → Follow up with related content
- Clicked but no conversion → Send case study/testimonial
- Multiple opens → High interest, priority for sales
- No open after 3 emails → Move to re-engagement
