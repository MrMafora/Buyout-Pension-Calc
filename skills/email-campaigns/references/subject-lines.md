# Subject Line Formulas for FedBuyOut

Tested subject line formulas optimized for federal employees and financial services.

---

## Formula Categories

### 1. Curiosity Gaps

Create information gaps that demand to be filled.

**Formula:** [Specific Number] + [Surprising Outcome] + [Question/Omission]

**Examples:**
- "3 Pension Mistakes Costing Federal Employees $50K+"
- "The Buyout Secret Most CSRS Employees Don't Know"
- "Why 73% of FERS Employees Regret This Decision"
- "The Pension Loophole They Don't Advertise"

**When to Use:**
- Educational emails
- Cold audiences
- Content-heavy emails

**Risk Level:** Medium (can feel clickbaity if overdone)

---

### 2. Personalization Patterns

Use recipient data to create relevance.

**Formula:** [Name] + [Relevant Trigger] + [Benefit/Question]

**Examples:**
- "{{firstName}}, Your Pension Window Is Closing"
- "{{firstName}}: Ready to Unlock Your Pension Value?"
- "For {{firstName}}: FERS Buyout Update"
- "Quick Question, {{firstName}}"

**When to Use:**
- Warm leads
- Re-engagement campaigns
- High-value segments

**Risk Level:** Low (but watch for over-personalization creepiness)

---

### 3. Urgency/Scarcity

Create time or availability pressure.

**Formula:** [Time Element] + [Consequence/Opportunity] + [Action]

**Examples:**
- "Rate Changes Coming: Lock In Your Buyout Value"
- "Only 4 Consultation Spots Left This Month"
- "48 Hours Left: Free Pension Valuation"
- "Before Rates Rise: Your Buyout Options"

**When to Use:**
- Limited-time offers
- Market-sensitive timing
- Final sequence emails

**Risk Level:** High (can trigger spam filters if overused)

---

### 4. Question-Based

Engage the reader's brain with direct questions.

**Formula:** [Direct Question] + [Implied Personal Relevance]

**Examples:**
- "Is Your Pension Worth More Than You Think?"
- "Should You Take the Monthly Checks?"
- "What's Your Pension Really Worth?"
- "Ready to Explore Your Buyout Options?"

**When to Use:**
- Early funnel education
- Re-engagement
- Consultation offers

**Risk Level:** Low

---

### 5. How-To Promises

Promise specific, valuable knowledge.

**Formula:** "How to" + [Achieve Desirable Outcome] + [Without/Even If]

**Examples:**
- "How to Maximize Your Pension Buyout Value"
- "How to Retire 5 Years Early (Legally)"
- "How to Access Your Pension Early Without Penalty"
- "How Federal Employees Build Wealth After Retirement"

**When to Use:**
- Educational content
- Lead magnets
- Blog post promotions

**Risk Level:** Low

---

### 6. List-Based

Promise scannable, valuable content.

**Formula:** [Number] + [Superlative/Adjective] + [Noun] + [Benefit]

**Examples:**
- "5 Ways to Increase Your Buyout Offer"
- "7 Tax Traps Every Pensioner Should Avoid"
- "3 Signs You Should Consider a Buyout"
- "10 Questions to Ask Before Selling Your Pension"

**When to Use:**
- Educational emails
- Nurture sequences
- Value-first content

**Risk Level:** Low

---

### 7. Contrarian/Pattern Interrupts

Challenge conventional wisdom.

**Formula:** "Why" + [Conventional Wisdom] + [Is Wrong/Incomplete]

**Examples:**
- "Why Monthly Pension Payments Aren't Always Best"
- "The Buyout Myth Federal Employees Believe"
- "Why Waiting Could Cost You Thousands"
- "Stop Thinking About Your Pension This Way"

**When to Use:**
- Re-engagement
- Breaking objections
- Thought leadership

**Risk Level:** Medium (must deliver on the premise)

---

### 8. Social Proof

Leverage what others are doing.

**Formula:** [Others Like You] + [Action Taken] + [Result/Benefit]

**Examples:**
- "Why 200+ Federal Employees Chose Buyouts Last Year"
- "The Pension Decision 89% of Our Clients Don't Regret"
- "What USPS Employees Are Doing With Their Pensions"
- "How Your Colleagues Are Maximizing Retirement"

**When to Use:**
- Middle/bottom funnel
- Case study promotions
- Trust-building

**Risk Level:** Low

## Subject Line Testing Framework

### A/B Test Rules

1. **Test One Variable at a Time**
   - Subject line only
   - Keep preview text, from name, send time constant

2. **Minimum Sample Size**
   - 100 recipients per variant minimum
   - 200+ for statistical significance

3. **Test Duration**
   - Wait 24 hours before calling winner
   - Account for time zones

4. **Winner Criteria**
   - Primary: Open rate
   - Secondary: Click rate (if tied)

### Test Ideas by Email Type

| Email Type | Test Approach |
|------------|---------------|
| Welcome | Personalization vs. Value proposition |
| Educational | Curiosity gap vs. How-to promise |
| Consultation Offer | Urgency vs. Question-based |
| Re-engagement | Direct question vs. Pattern interrupt |
| Final Notice | Hard urgency vs. Soft opt-out |

## Preview Text Strategy

### What It Is
- The snippet shown after subject in inbox
- Usually 40-100 characters
- Critical for mobile (where most emails are opened)

### Best Practices
1. **Complement the subject** - Don't repeat it
2. **Add context** - What will they learn?
3. **Create curiosity** - Why open?
4. **Include CTA hint** - What's next?

### Examples

**Subject:** "3 Pension Mistakes Costing Federal Employees $50K+"
**Bad Preview:** "Learn about pension mistakes and how to avoid them..."
**Good Preview:** "#2 is something 90% of FERS employees overlook →"

**Subject:** "{{firstName}}, Your Pension Window Is Closing"
**Bad Preview:** "We wanted to reach out about your pension situation..."
**Good Preview:** "Current rates favor buyouts. Calculate your value →"

- "15 minutes could change your retirement"

- "We miss you at FedBuyOut"
- "Is this still relevant to you?"
- "One last try: Pension resources"

## Subject Line Checklist

Before sending, verify:

- [ ] Under 60 characters (ideally under 50)
- [ ] No spam trigger words
- [ ] Clear value proposition or curiosity gap
- [ ] Matches email content
- [ ] Appropriate tone for audience
- [ ] Mobile-friendly (readable in 30-40 chars)
- [ ] Personalization tokens working correctly
- [ ] Preview text complements (not repeats)
- [ ] A/B test set up (if applicable)
- [ ] Spelling/grammar checked
