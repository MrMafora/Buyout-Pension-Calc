# Blog Outline Templates

## Standard Blog Structure

All blog posts should follow this general flow:

```
1. Hook/Lead (Problem or Promise)
2. Introduction (Context + Promise)
3. Body Content (3-5 main sections)
4. Conclusion (Summary + CTA)
5. Call-to-Action (Primary conversion)
```

---

## How-To Guide Outline

### Template Structure

```markdown
# [Compelling Title with Primary Keyword]

## Introduction (150-200 words)
- Hook: Relatable scenario or pain point
- Problem statement
- Promise of solution
- Brief credibility statement
- CTA #1 (Soft)

## What is [Topic]? (200-300 words)
- Definition in plain terms
- Why it matters to federal employees
- Context within federal benefits

## Prerequisites/Eligibility (150-200 words)
- Who this applies to
- Requirements checklist
- Common misconceptions

## Step-by-Step Guide (600-800 words)

### Step 1: [Action Name]
- Detailed instructions
- Tips for success
- Common mistakes to avoid

### Step 2: [Action Name]
- Detailed instructions
- Tips for success
- Resources needed

### Step 3: [Action Name]
- Detailed instructions
- What to expect
- Timeline

[Additional steps as needed]

## Pro Tips (150-200 words)
- Insider advice
- Lessons learned
- Best practices

## Common Mistakes to Avoid (200-300 words)
- Mistake 1: Explanation and fix
- Mistake 2: Explanation and fix
- Mistake 3: Explanation and fix

## What Happens Next (150-200 words)
- Timeline expectations
- Follow-up actions
- Where to get help

## Conclusion (100-150 words)
- Recap key points
- Reiterate value
- Encouraging closing
- CTA #2 (Primary)
```

```markdown
- What it is

- Quick pros/cons

- What it is
- Key features/benefits
- Ideal candidate profile
- Quick pros/cons

## Side-by-Side Comparison (Table format)
| Factor | Option A | Option B |
|--------|----------|----------|
| Eligibility | | |
| Payment/Benefit | | |
| Timeline | | |
| Flexibility | | |
| Tax Implications | | |

## Detailed Factor Analysis (400-500 words)

### Eligibility Requirements
- Compare side-by-side
- Edge cases

### Financial Impact
- Short-term
- Long-term
- Hidden costs

### Career Implications
- Future opportunities
- Limitations
```

## News/Analysis Outline

### Template Structure

```markdown

## Breaking News Summary (100-150 words)
- What happened
- Key facts (bullet points)
- Immediate impact
- Source citations

## Why This Matters (150-200 words)
- Who is affected
- Why federal employees should care
- Historical context
- Potential ripple effects

## Key Details and Changes (300-400 words)
- Detail 1: Explanation
- Detail 2: Explanation
- Detail 3: Explanation
- Direct quotes from sources

- Important dates
- Action windows
- Consequences of missing deadlines

## Impact Analysis by Employee Type (200-300 words)

- Mid-career employees
- Pre-retirement employees

- What's next
```

## Case Study Outline

### Template Structure

```markdown
# How [Name/Profile] Navigated [Topic]: A Federal Employee Success Story

## Introduction (150-200 words)
- Meet the subject (anonymized profile)
- Their situation at the start
- The challenge they faced
- Promise of insights to come
- CTA #1 (Soft)

## The Background (200-250 words)
- Subject's federal career
- Personal circumstances
- Financial situation
- Family considerations
- Why they considered [topic]

## The Decision Point (250-300 words)
- What triggered the decision
- Options considered
- Fears and concerns
- Information gathering process
- Turning point moment

## Taking Action (300-400 words)
- Step-by-step what they did
- Timeline of events
- Challenges encountered
- Support systems used
- Unexpected developments

## Obstacles and Solutions (200-250 words)
- Obstacle 1: How they overcame it
- Obstacle 2: How they overcame it
- Lessons learned from setbacks

## The Outcome (200-250 words)
- Where they are now
- Financial results
- Personal/professional results
- Would they do it differently?
- Advice for others

## Key Takeaways (200-250 words)
- Lesson 1
- Lesson 2
- Lesson 3
- Common thread insights

## Action Steps for Readers (150-200 words)
- How to apply these lessons
- Resources to use
- When to get help

## Conclusion (100 words)
- Inspiring closing
- Connection to reader
- Final CTA
```

## Outline Writing Tips

1. **Start with the end in mind**: Know your CTA before outlining
2. **Use the Rule of Three**: 3 main points, 3 examples, 3 steps
3. **Balance depth and length**: Every section should earn its place
4. **Include transition notes**: How sections connect
5. **Mark research needs**: [RESEARCH: OPM guidance on X]
6. **Note internal link opportunities**: [LINK: related post on Y]
7. **Plan CTAs strategically**: After intro (soft), mid-content (contextual), conclusion (primary)
