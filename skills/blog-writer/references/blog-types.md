# Blog Post Types Reference

## Overview

FedBuyOut content serves different purposes and audiences. Choose the right type based on user intent and business goals.

---

## How-To Guide {#how-to}

**Purpose**: Teach readers to accomplish a specific task

**Best For**
- Explaining processes (VSIP application, calculations)
- Step-by-step instructions
- Building trust through education
- SEO (high search volume for "how to" queries)

**Structure**
1. Hook with relatable problem
2. What they'll learn (preview)
3. Prerequisites/eligibility
4. Step-by-step instructions (3-7 steps)
5. Pro tips and warnings
6. Common mistakes to avoid
7. What happens next
8. Conclusion with CTA

**Example Topics**
- "How to Calculate Your VSIP Payment"
- "How to Apply for a Federal Buyout"
- "How to Decide Between VSIP and VERA"

**SEO Characteristics**
- Keywords: "how to [topic]", "[topic] guide"
- Featured snippet opportunities
- High intent but early-funnel

**Success Metrics**
- Time on page (5+ minutes ideal)
- Scroll depth (80%+ ideal)
- Low bounce rate
- High social shares

---

## Comparison Post {#comparison}

**Purpose**: Help readers choose between options

**Best For**
- VSIP vs VERA
- Buyout vs staying until retirement
- Different agency programs
- TSP withdrawal options

**Structure**
1. The decision dilemma (hook)
2. Option A overview (features, ideal for)
3. Option B overview (features, ideal for)
4. Side-by-side comparison table
5. Deep-dive factor analysis
6. Decision framework/questions
7. Real-world scenarios
8. Recommendation guidance
9. Conclusion with CTA

**Example Topics**
- "VSIP vs VERA: Which Is Right for You?"
- "Federal Buyout vs RIF: Choosing Your Path"
- "FEHB vs Private Insurance After Federal Service"

**SEO Characteristics**
- Keywords: "[A] vs [B]", "[A] or [B]", "difference between"
- Comparison tables perform well
- Often mid-funnel (decision-making stage)

**Success Metrics**
- High engagement (comments/questions)
- Multiple page views (comparing with other posts)
- Strong CTA click-through

---

## News/Analysis {#news}

**Purpose**: Inform readers about timely developments

**Best For**
- OPM policy updates
- Agency announcements
- Legislative changes
- Deadline alerts
- Budget implications

**Structure**
1. Breaking news summary (TL;DR)
2. Why this matters
3. Key details and changes
4. Timeline/deadlines
5. Impact by employee type
6. Actions to take
7. Expert analysis/context
8. Looking ahead
9. Conclusion with urgency CTA

**Example Topics**
- "New OPM Guidance: What Federal Employees Need to Know"
- "Budget Cuts Announced: Buyout Implications"
- "VSIP Deadline Extended: What This Means for You"

**SEO Characteristics**
- Keywords: "breaking", "new", "2024", "update"
- Time-sensitive (traffic spikes then declines)
- Newsjacking opportunities
- Often linked from other content

**Success Metrics**
- Quick traffic spikes
- Social shares
- Return visitor rate
- Email signups (alert subscriptions)

---

## Case Study {#case-study}

**Purpose**: Demonstrate real results and build credibility

**Best For**
- Success stories
- Complex decision examples
- Overcoming objections
- Social proof

**Structure**
1. Meet the subject (relatable profile)
2. Background and situation
3. The challenge/decision point
4. The process (step-by-step)
5. Obstacles encountered
6. The outcome (quantified if possible)
7. Lessons learned
8. Key takeaways for readers
9. Action steps
10. Conclusion with CTA

**Example Topics**
- "How Sarah Turned Her VSIP Into a Thriving Business"
- "From Fed to Free: Mark's Buyout Journey"
- "The Buyout Decision: A 20-Year Employee's Story"

**SEO Characteristics**
- Keywords: "success story", "case study", "how [persona] [result]"
- Long-tail variations
- Often featured in roundups

**Success Metrics**
- High time on page
- Strong emotional response (comments)
- High conversion rate
- Referral traffic

---

## Quick Reference/FAQ {#faq}

**Purpose**: Answer specific questions concisely

**Best For**
- Specific factual questions
- Definition posts
- Quick answers
- Featured snippet optimization

**Structure**
1. Direct answer to question (40-60 words)
2. Detailed explanation
3. Related questions/considerations
4. Examples if applicable
5. Link to deeper resources

**Example Topics**
- "What Is VSIP?"
- "How Is VSIP Taxed?"
- "Can You Work After Taking VSIP?"

**SEO Characteristics**
- Keywords: question format
- Featured snippet optimization
- "People also ask" targeting
- Often supports pillar content

**Success Metrics**
- Featured snippet capture
- Low bounce rate (answered question)
- High CTR from search
- Internal link clicks

---

## List Post {#list}

**Purpose**: Provide scannable, comprehensive information

**Best For**
- Tips and strategies
- Mistakes to avoid
- Resources/tools
- Checklists

**Structure**
1. Hook with problem/promise
2. Brief intro to list
3. Numbered items (5-12 ideal)
4. Each item: title + 100-200 words
5. Quick recap
6. Conclusion with CTA

**Example Topics**
- "7 Smart Ways to Use Your VSIP Payment"
- "10 Mistakes to Avoid When Considering a Buyout"
- "5 Signs You're Ready for a Federal Buyout"

**SEO Characteristics**
- Keywords: "[number] ways to", "best [topic]", "mistakes to avoid"
- High shareability
- Easy to scan

**Success Metrics**
- Social shares
- Bookmark rate
- Return visits
- Link building

## Choosing the Right Type

### By User Intent

| Intent | Best Type |
|--------|-----------|
| Learn how to do something | How-To Guide |
| Compare options | Comparison |
| Stay informed | News/Analysis |
| See proof/results | Case Study |
| Get quick answer | FAQ/Quick Reference |
| Browse/scan | List Post |

### By Funnel Stage

| Stage | Best Types |
|-------|------------|
| Awareness | News, List posts |
| Interest | How-To, FAQ |
| Consideration | Comparison, Case Study |
| Decision | Case Study, Comparison |

### By Business Goal

| Goal | Best Types |
|------|------------|
| Drive traffic | News, List posts |
| Build authority | How-To, Case Study |
| Generate leads | How-To, Comparison |
| Convert prospects | Case Study, Comparison |
| Support SEO | FAQ, How-To |
